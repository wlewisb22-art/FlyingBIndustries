— 01 / Why UV?

Not your standard printed sticker.

UV-printed decals are a step up from typical inkjet or laser-printed stickers. Here's why it matters.

Durability

Cured under UV light, instantly

The ink isn't soaked into the substrate or sprayed on top — it's polymerized solid the moment it lands. The result is a layer that resists scratching, fading, peeling, and chemical exposure. Boat decals stay readable in the sun for years. Cooler decals survive being beaten up at the lake.

White Ink Capability

Bright colors on dark surfaces

Most sticker printing can't print white — it relies on the white of the paper showing through. UV printing lays down opaque white ink, so a red-and-yellow logo on a matte black tumbler or a charcoal cooler shows up bright and accurate, not muddy and washed out.

— 05 / Common Questions

Things people ask us.

How long will the decal last outdoors?

Outdoor-rated UV-printed vinyl typically lasts 3–5 years in full sun and weather without significant fading. Indoor or covered applications easily last 7+ years. The biggest enemy is direct, prolonged UV exposure combined with extreme temperature swings.

Are these really waterproof?

Yes — fully waterproof. The cured UV ink and the vinyl itself are both impervious to water. You can pressure-wash a boat with our decals on it, run a cooler through the dishwasher (if you wanted to), or have them out in the rain indefinitely.

Will it work on a dark surface like a black cooler?

Yes — we print white ink as a base layer under your colors when needed. This is something many sticker printers can't do. The result is full-color accuracy on black, navy, dark green, or any dark substrate.

What surfaces don't they stick to?

Smooth, clean, non-porous surfaces are best (metal, glass, plastic, painted surfaces, fiberglass, hard plastic coolers). They won't adhere well to fabric, soft rubber, very porous wood, or surfaces with silicone residue. If unsure, ask before ordering.

How do I apply them?

Clean the surface with isopropyl alcohol, peel the backing, place carefully, and squeegee out air bubbles. Larger decals (over 6") we recommend using the wet method with a soap-water mist for repositioning. We can include application instructions with your order.

Can I remove them later?

Standard adhesive is permanent — you can remove it, but it takes work (heat from a hair dryer, slow peel, residue cleaner). If you need removable decals (for rentals, temporary signage), tell us up front and we'll use removable adhesive.

Do you do laminated decals?

Yes, we can add a protective laminate over the print for extra abrasion resistance — useful for hard hats, tool boxes, and anywhere the decal will see physical wear. Just let us know.

What artwork files work best?

Vector files (SVG, AI, EPS, PDF) are ideal — they scale to any size without losing quality. High-resolution PNG or JPG also work well. For photos, the higher resolution the better.
