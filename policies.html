The Fine Print, Made Plain

Proofs, returns, and peace of mind.

How we handle your orders, your information, and the small stuff. We aim to keep it simple and human — and we apply the same policies to every service: golf balls, tumblers, patches, decals, and custom work alike.

— Core Policies

The three things to know first.

Digital Proof Before Production

Before we engrave, print, or produce anything, we send you a digital mockup showing exactly how your design will appear on the finished product. Nothing goes into production until you approve the layout. If something doesn't look right, just say so — we'll adjust until it does.

Returns on Custom Orders

Because every order is custom-made, approved orders are generally not returnable. The proof-approval step is what protects you here — once you've signed off on the design, we begin work on materials that can't be repurposed for anyone else.

That said, we stand behind our work. If there's a production error, a quality issue, or the finished product doesn't match the proof you approved, contact us and we'll make it right — repair, replacement, or refund as appropriate to the situation.

Order Changes & Cancellations

Need to change quantity, swap a design, or cancel? Reach out as soon as possible. Changes and cancellations are usually possible before you approve the proof — once production begins, options are more limited.

Shipping & Delivery

We ship throughout the United States. Once your order is shipped, you'll receive a tracking number by email. Standard shipping is via USPS or UPS, with the carrier chosen based on package size and your location.

Delivery times vary by carrier and destination, typically 3–7 business days within the continental US after shipping. Expedited options are available — let us know if you have a hard deadline and we'll work something out.

If a package is lost or damaged in transit, we'll work with you and the carrier to resolve it (file claims, replace the order if confirmed lost, etc.). International orders are by request only — please contact us before placing one.

Color & Proof Accuracy

Colors may appear slightly different on the finished product than they do on your screen. This is due to monitor calibration, the printing or engraving process, and the way ink or laser interacts with the material's surface. The digital proof we send is the closest representation of how the final product will look.

If exact color matching is critical for your project (for example, matching a specific corporate brand color), let us know in your initial email so we can discuss whether we can accommodate it.

Image Quality & Resolution

The quality of the finished engraving or print depends entirely on the quality of the image you provide. Low-resolution, blurry, or pixelated source files will produce low-resolution, blurry, or pixelated results — there's no way to add detail that wasn't there to begin with.

For best results: send the highest-resolution PNG or JPG you have. For logos and graphics, vector files (SVG, AI, EPS) or PNGs with transparent backgrounds are ideal. For photos, original camera or phone files are better than screenshots or images saved from social media.

If we have concerns about whether your file will produce a good result, we'll let you know before sending the proof so you have a chance to provide a better version.

Damaged Shipments

If your shipment arrives visibly damaged, please contact us within 7 days of delivery with photos of the packaging and the damaged contents. This helps us file a claim with the carrier and arrange a replacement quickly.

Reports made after 7 days are harder to resolve because most carriers require timely claims, but we'll always do our best to help.

Limitation of Liability

In the event of a quality issue, production error, or other problem with your order, our maximum liability is the total amount you paid for the affected portion of the order.

Flying B Industries is not responsible for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages — for example, costs related to events, gifts, or other purposes the order was intended for. We do everything we can to deliver on time and as expected, but ultimately the finished product is what we're responsible for.

Right to Decline Orders

We reserve the right to decline any order for any reason, including but not limited to: requests involving copyrighted or trademarked content without proper authorization, content we consider offensive or inappropriate, files we cannot produce to our quality standards, materials we don't safely work with, or requests we're unable to fulfill on the requested timeline.

If we decline an order, we'll let you know promptly and refund any payment that's already been made.

Production Delays & Force Majeure

Most orders ship on schedule, but production timelines can occasionally be affected by factors outside our control: material availability from suppliers, shipping carrier delays, weather events, equipment issues, or other unforeseen circumstances.

If a delay affects your order, we'll communicate as soon as we're aware. For time-sensitive orders, please share your deadline up front so we can confirm whether we can meet it before you commit.

Cookies & Analytics

This website uses two analytics tools — Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity — to understand how visitors find and use the site.

Google Analytics sets cookies that collect anonymous usage data: pages viewed, approximate location, device and browser type, traffic source, and how visitors interact with page elements (such as button clicks).

Microsoft Clarity records anonymous session activity — mouse movements, scrolls, clicks, and a visual heatmap of how visitors use the page — so we can see what's working and what's confusing. Clarity automatically masks any text input fields, so anything someone types is never recorded.

No personally identifying information is collected through either tool. We use this data only to improve the site and decide which features are working. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using the official Google opt-out browser add-on, and you can opt out of Microsoft Clarity at clarity.microsoft.com.

Governing Law

These terms and any orders placed with Flying B Industries are governed by the laws of the State of Georgia, United States. Any disputes will be resolved in Paulding County, Georgia, and both parties agree to that jurisdiction.

— Your Privacy

How we handle your information.

What we collect. When you email us a request, we receive whatever you choose to send — typically your name, email address, phone number, shipping address, the artwork or images you want printed or engraved, and details about your order.

What we use it for. We use your information for one purpose only: producing and delivering your custom order and communicating with you about it. That's it.

What we don't do. We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your information with any third parties. We do not add you to marketing lists. We do not send promotional emails or advertisements. We do not use your contact information to follow up with offers, and we do not share your artwork or order details with anyone outside of fulfilling your specific order.

Your images and artwork. The artwork, logos, and photos you send remain yours. We use them only to produce your order. We do not display, share, or reuse your images for marketing, portfolio promotion, or any other purpose without your explicit permission.

Records. We keep order records so we can support reorders, address any quality concerns, and meet basic business and tax requirements. If you ever want your information removed from our records, just email us and we'll take care of it.

Website analytics. We use Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity to understand how visitors use the site. Both tools collect only anonymous usage data — neither captures your name, email, or anything you type. We never connect website analytics to specific orders or customer identities. See the "Cookies & Analytics" section above for full details.

Service providers. The only third parties involved in your order are the ones required to fulfill it — for example, our shipping carrier, or, if you ask us to source materials (a specific brand of tumbler, specific golf balls, hats, etc.), the supplier we order from. They receive only the information needed to do their part (like a shipping address) and nothing more.

Questions. If you have any questions about how we handle your information, just ask: orders@flyingbindustries.com
