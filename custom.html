— 03 / How Custom Works

The process.

One-offs and small batches follow the same rhythm as the rest of our work.

01

Email the idea

Tell us what you want to make, what material or item it'll be on, and roughly when you need it. Reference photos help. Don't worry about being technical — describe it in your own words.

02

We figure out feasibility & cost

We'll come back with what's possible, which method (laser vs UV) makes sense, and what it'll cost — including whether you should send the item or have us source it.

03

Proof, produce, ship

Once you approve the design and the quote, we make it, photograph the result, and ship it. Custom one-offs typically turn around in 5–10 business days.

— 04 / Common Questions

Things people ask us.

What can't you work with?

A few materials we avoid: PVC and vinyl release toxic fumes when lasered (we don't laser them, but we can UV print on most). Reflective polished metals (mirror-finish chrome, untreated stainless) are tricky for our laser — anodized or powder-coated versions work better. Anything with copyrighted designs we don't own. Beyond that, send it our way and we'll let you know.

Can I send you an item to engrave instead of sourcing one?

Absolutely — it's often the best approach for custom one-offs. Email us first so we can confirm the material will work, then ship it to us. We'll engrave it and ship it back.

Do you do single-item orders?

Yes. A wedding gift, a memorial piece, an award for one person — all welcome. We don't have a minimum order. Pricing reflects the setup time for one-off work, but we keep it reasonable.

How do you decide between laser and UV?

Quick rule: if you want a clean monochrome look with permanent etched detail, that's laser. If you want full color, photos, or designs with multiple colors, that's UV. We'll always recommend the better method for your specific project.

Can you replicate a sample I've seen?

Usually yes, as long as it's not a copyrighted design or branded product. Send us a photo or link and we'll let you know whether we can match the look. Note that small variations in technique and material may produce slight differences.

What if I'm not sure what I want yet?

That's fine — we're happy to talk it through. Email us with a rough idea (occasion, recipient, budget, vibe) and we'll suggest some directions. No commitment to order just to brainstorm.

How big can you engrave or print?

Laser work is limited to roughly 24" x 12" per pass — larger items may need multiple passes or tile-matching. UV print bed is similar. For anything bigger, ask and we'll explore the options.

Do you ever say no?

Sometimes. Trademarked artwork without authorization, requests that are technically impossible for our equipment, materials that can't be safely worked, and timelines we can't realistically meet — those we'll decline up front rather than disappoint you. Most things, though, we can do.
